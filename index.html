problem statement

Since the early 20th century, minorities have been underrepresented in the Hollywood film industry due to a sense of familiarity towards white, male actors, resulting in less diversity and more white-led motion pictures. Only 20% of Oscar winners in the past decade were awarded to people of color.

harmful effects on minorities

Not only does the lack of diversity deter non-white actors and actresses from breaking into the industry, but it also determines how people view minorities, which may include negative stereotypes. As a result, people of color have less of a chance to share their culture and stories through motion pictures.

Furthermore, movie studios such as Marvel and DC have many cases of whitewashing their own characters in movies. One popular example is Wanda Maximoff, who is originally Romani in the comics but is portrayed by Elizabeth Olsen, a white actress. Another wide-known example is Ra's al Ghul from Christopher Nolan's Batman trilogy. Although he is described as Arabic in the comics, he is represented in the live-action by Liam Neeson, another white actor.

disparity within award shows

In a census conducted on all nominations in the Oscars from 1928 to 2020, an estimated 6% of winners were people of color, while the rest predominantly consisted of white individuals.

Moreover, men were nominated four times as much as women in the Oscars, which includes categories such as Best Director, Writing, and Production Design.

There was a greater disparity in major award categories when comparing to their support roles. For example, Black women won 9.52% of awards in the Best Actress in a Support Role category, but they only won 1.08% of awards in the Best Actress category. This could entail that leading roles tended to be given to white individuals rather than people of color.

From 1928-2020, the amount of female winners each year were <15.In addition, every race, other than white, won <10 awards each year. These statistics were consistent for more than nine decades.

go further

We are the future, and it is up to us and our generation to actively make a change in the film industry. Check out the links below to see how you can join and get involved with film. We hope to provide resources through incentives and donation platforms.

  • Women In Film Fellowship

    The Women in Film organization strives to get more women into the film industry, whether it be working on screen or behind the scenes. In this year-long fellowship, participants (producers, crew, directors, and so much more) will have the opportunity to meet with mentors in their field.

  • Against the Grain Artistic Scholarship

    The Against the Grain Artistic Scholarship is a scholarship that provides academic aid exclusively for those of at least 25% Asian or Pacific Islander descent who want to pursue a major in film. This scholarship is currently not open as of August 2023, but we highly recommend checking back in January 2024 when the applications open.

  • Islamic Scholarship Fund

    The Islamic Scholarship Fund is an organization that aims to enter more American Muslims into the film and television industry by providing scholarships that range from $3,000 to $5,000. Although the applications for the scholarship are currently closed 2023, it will re-open for 2024.

  • New York Women in Film and Television

    The New York Women in Film and Television (NYWIFT) scholarship aims to help women in the New York film community gain access to government and foundation funding for film projects. There is no application fee to apply, but in order to apply you do have to be a member of the NYWIFT. The applications are always open.
